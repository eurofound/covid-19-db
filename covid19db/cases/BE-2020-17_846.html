Factsheet for case BE-2020-17/846 – measures in Belgium

Metal & technology sector agreement on the application of the generic guide "safe at work" to prevent the spread of COVID-19 at work

Background information

The Generic Guide 'Safe at Work' will play an important role in the restart of companies. This will be the key to restart many companies in the technology and metal sector as soon as they are allowed to. Using tips and good examples, the guide aims to be a breakthrough for a step-by-step economic restart in healthy and safe conditions. This means, among other things, keeping enough distance on the work floor and, if necessary, protecting employees with mouth masks. These are building blocks in which employees and their colleagues can work as safely as possible as soon as they are allowed to. The use of the guide has been formalised through a sectoral agreement.

Content of measure

The guide applies to all companies within the metal and technological sector, namely those in the relevant joint committees 111 and 209. Enterprises must comply at all times with the decisions taken and measures imposed by the government to limit the spread of the COVID-19 virus. In addition there is the intersectoral generic guide that was drafted by the social partners in collaboration with the governmental services. This sector agreement should be read together with this generic guide. It involves a chapter on the importance of social dialogue and communication in the workplace, risk analysis, attention for the health of employees, attention for psychosocial support for employees and a procedure for preventive measures.

Use of measure

The guide is specifically meant for all companies that are part of the joint committees (JC) in the metal and technological sector, namely JC 111 (for blue collar workers) and JC 209 (for white collar workers). These are significant and large joint committees with several important and prolific companies in industry. It is difficult to measure if all companies adhere to these rules and unclear if any controls will be held by the sector itself.
